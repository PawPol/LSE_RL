# Phase IV-A Margin Mismatch Diagnostic

Generated by `scripts/phase4_margin_mismatch.py` (inline).

## Summary

Both current Phase IV-A families fail the informative-replay gate for the same structural reason: the calibrated schedule demands a design-point margin `u_ref_used / |beta|` that is 3-15x larger than the realized `|r_t - V*(s'_t)|`, and simultaneously the scheduled `|beta|` is in the 10^-4 range, so `u = beta * margin` stays below the 5e-3 activation threshold regardless of which margin-rich transitions we condition on. For `chain_sparse_credit_0`: mean `|beta|`=0.000247, realized Q75(|margin|)=4.97, design margin=17.3, gap ratio=0.288. For `grid_hazard_1`: mean `|beta|`=0.000154, realized Q75(|margin|)=0.94, design margin=12.5, gap ratio=0.0752. The diagnosis is not "margin is tiny" in absolute terms (realized margins hit 1-5 in chain_sparse_credit) but "margin is tiny relative to the schedule". Equivalently, the A_t surrogate (per-stage Bellman-residual scale) inferred from the pilot is ~10-27, so the calibrator shrinks `|beta|` to keep `beta*A_t` inside the trust region; but realized per-transition margins are bounded by `reward_bound + V_max ~ 1+gamma*V_max`, which is much smaller than A_t in these reward-sparse MDPs. Net effect: the informative stages pass at the schedule level, but replay never realises design-size margins on individual transitions.

## What needs to change in Phase IV-A2

- **A_t / realized-margin ratio must be O(1), not O(10).** The calibrator sets `|beta_t| = u_ref_used / A_t` to keep trust-region step size under control. In the current families A_t ~= design_margin >> realized Q75(|margin|), so `u = beta * margin` is quenched. Phase IV-A2 families need A_t (the per-stage Bellman-residual scale estimated by the calibrator) to be comparable to the per-transition `|r_t - V*(s')|` distribution it is supposed to represent. Practically this means either (i) taming A_t by bounding the spread of `r_t + gamma*V(s')` across samples, or (ii) inflating realized margins with reward heterogeneity.
- **Dense, spread-out reward structure.** `chain_sparse_credit` has step_cost=-0.01 and a single terminal reward; `grid_hazard` has step_cost=0 and a 0.3-prob hazard transition. In both cases per-transition margins concentrate on a small set of modes (V_max on most transitions, a spike on the few hazard/terminal ones). Replace with intermediate shaped rewards, per-step stochastic rewards, or multi-tier reward states so that `r_t - V*(s')` is spread across a continuum and its Q75 is not dwarfed by its A_t surrogate.
- **Guard the `|beta|` floor.** The current schedules produce `|beta|` in the 1e-4 range at every stage. With a 5e-3 threshold and realized margins bounded by `reward_bound` (=1.0) plus `gamma*V_max`, `u = beta*margin` cannot cross threshold unless `|beta| >= 5e-3 / |margin|_Q75`, which here would require `|beta| >= 1e-3`. Tasks that force `|beta|` below ~1e-3 on all stages are structurally incompatible with the Option-C informative-replay gate.
- **Design for margin-up, not margin-down.** Paradoxically, a "more stochastic" MDP can *shrink* realized margins when V*(s') averages out state uncertainty (grid_hazard: `|margin|` median ~0). Favour deterministic-but-heterogeneous rewards or bandit-like stages where `r_t` carries real signal relative to `V*(s')`.

## Phase IV-A2 Requirements

Concrete, measurable gates for a Phase IV-A2 family (tested on the same 1000-episode counterfactual replay):

1. **Gap ratio >= 0.5.** Realized `Q75(|r_t - V*(s'_t)|)` over the full replay must be at least half of the mean design-point margin `u_ref_used / |beta|` across stages with `u_ref_used > 0`. Current values: chain_sparse_credit=0.288, grid_hazard=0.075.
2. **|beta| floor.** Mean `|beta_used|` across stages must satisfy `mean|beta| * Q75(|margin|) >= 5e-3`, i.e. a single typical transition on an informative stage actually crosses the activation threshold. Current values: chain_sparse_credit mean|beta|*Q75|m|=0.00123 (need >= 5e-3), grid_hazard=0.000145.
3. **Per-stage realization rate.** On informative stages, at least 50% of stages must have `Q75(|u|) >= u_ref_used`. Current: chain_sparse_credit=0.06, grid_hazard=0.09.
4. **Reward heterogeneity along trajectories.** Per-transition `r_t` must have non-trivial variance across stages so that `|r_t - V*(s'_t)|` is spread (not pinned near `-V_max`). Concretely, target std(r_t)/reward_bound >= 0.3 and std(|margin|)/mean(|margin|) in [0.5, 2.0]. Current std(r)/reward_bound: chain_sparse_credit=0.34, grid_hazard=0.30 - the spread is acceptable; the issue is the *shape* (long right tail from V*) rather than the spread per se.
5. **Design consistency check.** The calibrator's `A_t` surrogate must track per-transition `|margin|` within a factor of ~3, not a factor of ~10. In both current families `A_t ~= design_margin ~= 10-27` while `Q75|margin| ~= 1-5`, i.e. a 3-15x mismatch.
6. **Keep p_align away from the boundary.** On at least half of informative stages, `p_align = P(margin > 0)` must lie in [0.3, 0.7]. When p_align saturates (all positive or all negative), the `xi_ref * sqrt(p_align)` informative-mass diagnostic is structurally muted.

Practical recipe for a passing family:

- Start from a dense-reward MDP (per-step rewards drawn from a non-degenerate distribution, e.g. `r_t ~ U[-1, 1]` or a shaped tier).
- Moderate horizon (T=20) so `V*(s')` does not dominate `r_t` uniformly.
- Verify pre-selection on the same counterfactual-replay harness: compute `mean|beta|` and `Q75|margin|` from a 1000-episode pilot *before* accepting into the suite, and require the products above.
